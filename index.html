How to use this

A few patterns that make this prompt dramatically more useful.

01 Always fill in the header fields first. CONTENT TYPE, CONTENT LENGTH, and SOURCE CREDIBILITY change how the AI weights everything. A founder's podcast is read differently than a peer-reviewed paper.
02 Paste the full transcript — never summarize it first. If you pre-compress, you lose the examples and stories that make the notes real. The AI needs the raw material.
03 Run the Ghost Notes lens separately after the base run for richer divergent thinking. Use the stochastic-multi-agent-consensus skill to spawn 5 agents on the ghost notes prompt and aggregate the divergent reads.
04 Stack lenses sequentially. Run Base first → then pipe the output into the Obsidian vault lens to convert it to atomic notes. Or run Venture first → then Ghost notes only on the Part 2 output to push the opportunities further.
05 Section 2I (7-day actions) is the most important section. If nothing moves from a learning session into a concrete action, the notes don't compound. Always use this section before closing the conversation.
06 For StreakMate pitch research: run the StreakMate lens on investor talks, YC essays, and sales tech competitor announcements weekly. The [STREAKMATE SIGNAL] flags surface the relevant signal without reading everything.
07 Section 2K (content quality score) is your calibration check. Before acting on section 2I, read 2K. If the signal-to-noise is low or the author's credibility is weak, downgrade how aggressively you act.
08 Section 2L (cross-field connections) generates your best Obsidian links. The ideas that connect across domains — psychology into sales, physics into business strategy — are the ones worth building permanent notes around.
